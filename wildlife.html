Minneriya National Park

Minneriya National Park is located in the north central province of Sri Lanka and is one of the country's smaller parks, covering slightly over 300 square miles. Despite its short size, the reserve contains a diverse range of habitats, including grasslands and heavy scrub, dry tropical forest, and wetlands. During the dry months, Minneriya also has a man-made tank that filters water to the reservoir and nourishes the park's fauna. Deer, purple-faced langur and macaque monkeys, sloth bears, and over 20 leopards have been spotted in Minneriya, as well as a diverse range of migratory and indigenous birds like as the hanging parrot, brown-capped babbler, and green bee-eater. During the dry season (July – October), however, the reserve's headline attraction is 'The Gathering,' a spectacle in which over 300 elephants assemble at the Minneriya reservoir in quest of water and new grass. This beautiful event is the world's largest gathering of Asian elephants.

Path To Minneriya National Park

Dambulla is about 200 kilometers from Colombo, and the park can be reached via Dambulla. If coming from Colombo, take Kandy Road or Negambo Road to Kurunegala. The Dambulla - Habarana Road can be reached from Kurunegala. Turn right to Minneriya from the Habarana intersection.

Horton Plains

The Horton Plains, located in the central highlands, rise to an astonishing 2,100 meters above sea level, making it Sri Lanka's highest plateau and earning it the nickname "Roof of the Island." Cloud forest and lush montane grasslands dominate the plains, which are rich in indigenous species and animals that have adapted to the chilly temperature. While the elephant vanished from the Horton Plains in the 1940s, the area now supports a diverse range of animals, birds, amphibians, and reptiles. The sambar deer, which wanders in vast groups and skirts over the high grasses, is the most common creature seen. Some of the 24 animal species found here include giant squirrels, wild boars, rusty-spotted cats, and purple-faced langurs, as well as the Horton Plains slender loris, one of the world's most endangered primates. The cloud forests are home to rare reptile species like the rhino-horned lizard, as well as a diverse assortment of migratory and endemic birds for bird watchers.

Path To Horton Plains

At a distance of 10 km from Ohiya, 29 km from Nuwara Eliya and 48 km from Ella, Horton Plains National Park is a protected area in the central highlands of Sri Lanka. It is one of the most beautiful World Heritage Sites in the world and among the prime places to visit in Nuwara Eliya during Sri Lanka Tour.

Gal Oya National Park

Sri Lanka's overlooked national park, established in 1954, is mostly unspoilt and under-visited, making it a more real and unforgettable experience. Senanayake Samudraya Lake, the country's largest inland body of water, is framed by lush evergreen forest and open savannah, and is dotted with little islands. It is the only area in Sri Lanka where you can go on a boat safari or explore on foot or by jeep. Gal Oya National Park is known for being one of the best sites in the world to witness Asian elephants in their native habitat, and watching these magnificent creatures swim from island to island across Senanayake Samudraya Lake is a highlight of the reserve. Over 150 different bird species may be seen in the park, and in the middle of the lake is Birds' Island, where exotic colonies of birds compete for breeding space. At Gal Oya, you can witness wild boar, deer, leopards, sloth bears, and mugger crocodiles.

Path To Gal Oya National Park

From Colombo, travel through Ratnapura, Pelmadulla, Udawalawe, Thanamalwila, Wellawaya, Moneragala, and then north from Siyambalanduwa to Inginiyagala to reach Gal Oya National Park. Inginiyagala, 20 kilometers west of Ampara, is the park's entrance.
