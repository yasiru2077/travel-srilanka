enjoy food

Though Sri Lankan cuisine bears similarities to South Indian cuisine, it is nonetheless distinct in its own right. Sri Lanka's cuisine culture has evolved into a blend of varied curry recipes and scrumptious dishes as a result of years of colonization and influence from other countries. There are some facts that can be declared with confidence about Sri Lankan cuisine: Sri Lankans adore spices, they adore food that bursts with flavor, and many of them appreciate deep-fried, delectable treats. Your mouth will delight with happiness no matter what you eat in Sri Lanka.

Because Sri Lanka is a tropical island, coconuts and fish are two of the most important ingredients in Sri Lankan cuisine. Fish is cooked in curries, and coconut is a common element in cookery in some form or another. Sri Lankans eat a lot of rice and curry, but they also eat a lot of bread, both roti style flatbreads and loaves of bread. more info

go for a hike

For many visitors, Sri Lanka is synonymous with lovely, blonde beaches, but the harsh, mountainous interior holds a different world. Vehicles replaced hiking as the primary mode of transportation on this beautiful tropical island only after railways were built across the highlands. Within striking distance of the shore, modern-day trekkers can reach a world of rocky ruins, religious places, spectacular views, and falling tea farms. Because of Sri Lanka's small size the island is only 268 miles long and 139 miles wide trekkers don't have to worry about intricate logistics. For walks in national parks you'll need a guide, but most hikes are easy day hikes where you can just show up and start walking. more info

Discover the genuine meaning of culture.

Sri Lankan culture has been influenced by a variety of forces, but it has managed to retain many of its old characteristics. Its ancient history and Buddhist background have largely affected it. The country has a long and illustrious artistic legacy that includes music, dance, and visual arts. Cuisine, festivals, and sports all reflect the Sri Lankan way of life. South Indian influences can be seen in a variety of ways. The Portuguese, the Dutch, and the British all had a hand in colonizing the area. Cricket, food, holistic health, religious icons like the Buddhist flag, and cultural exports like tea, cinnamon, and diamonds are all well-known in Sri Lankan culture. The culture of Sri Lanka is diverse, as it differs from region to region.

Sri Lanka has a long history of human settlement, dating back over two millennia, and its civilisation is heavily influenced by that of the Indian subcontinent. The island's two primary ethnic groups, the Sinhalese and Tamils, as well as the island's two dominant religions, Buddhism and Hinduism, all came from India, and Indian influence can be found in sectors as diverse as art, architecture, literature, music, medicine, and astronomy. more info
