Galle fort

The Galle Dutch Fort, located on Sri Lanka's southern coast, is one of the country's most prized historical sites. The Portuguese built the fort during their control in Sri Lanka, and it was later repaired by the Dutch. The fort has immense cultural and architectural worth due to its amazing history. As a result, it has been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in order to protect the natural beauty of the area. For hundreds of years, it was also an important trading port! The stunning historic buildings have been turned into trendy boutiques, cafes, and restaurants in recent years. You could spend a full day experiencing the magnificence of Galle Fort, from shopping to history to sunset at the fort's wall.
